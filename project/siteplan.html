Digital Horizon E-Library

Site Plan Document

Site Name

Digital Horizon E-Library

This name was selected to convey a sense of limitless possibilities and forward-thinking innovation in digital reading. "Digital Horizon" suggests the vast expanse of knowledge available through the platform, while "E-Library" clearly communicates the service offered. The name appeals to modern readers seeking convenient access to digital books and resources.

Optional domain: digitalhorizon-library.com

Site Purpose

Digital Horizon E-Library serves as a comprehensive online library platform that provides users with access to a diverse collection of digital books, audiobooks, and educational resources. The site will feature:

  • A searchable catalog of over 15+ digital titles across multiple genres
  • User account management with borrowing history tracking
  • Book recommendations based on reading preferences
  • Membership registration and contact form
  • Reading lists and collections curated by librarians
  • Information about library services and hours

The platform aims to make reading more accessible by providing 24/7 access to quality literature and educational materials from any device.

Scenarios

Scenario 1: New Reader Discovery

"I'm looking for science fiction books published in the last year. How can I find new releases in my favorite genre?"

The site will provide advanced filtering options in the catalog page, allowing users to sort by genre, publication date, author, and popularity ratings. Each book will display key information including publication year, genre tags, author details, and user ratings.

Scenario 2: Membership Information

"What are the benefits of becoming a premium member, and how do I sign up?"

The membership page will clearly outline different membership tiers, their benefits (such as simultaneous borrowing limits, early access to new releases, and audiobook access), pricing information, and a straightforward registration form to join.

Color Schema

Usage: Body text, secondary headings (h3, h4), paragraph content, footer background

Light Gray

#F5F5F5

Usage: Section backgrounds, borders, subtle dividers, card shadows

Typography

Heading Font: Playfair Display

This elegant serif font will be used for all headings (h1, h2, h3) to convey sophistication and literary tradition. It provides excellent readability while maintaining a classic, bookish aesthetic.

Technical Notes

  • Data Source: Books will be fetched from a local JSON file or the Open Library API
  • Dynamic Features: Book catalog with filtering, modal dialogs for book details, local storage for user preferences and reading lists
  • Forms: Membership registration form with validation
  • Responsive Design: Mobile-first approach with breakpoints at 768px and 1024px
  • Accessibility: Semantic HTML, ARIA labels, keyboard navigation support
